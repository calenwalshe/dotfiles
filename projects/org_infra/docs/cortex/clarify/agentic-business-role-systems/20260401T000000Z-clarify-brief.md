## Idea (verbatim)

> can you please check and see - do you actually have any info about systems that people are building agentically to essentially run business with different agent roles playing different roles to capture task etc...?

---

## Goal

Understand the current landscape of multi-agent orchestration systems designed to operate business functions autonomously, where distinct agents hold defined roles (e.g. sales agent, ops agent, task router), so that an informed decision can be made about whether to research, adopt, or build such a system.

---

## Non-Goals

- Does not cover single-agent automation (RPA, single-LLM chatbots, basic assistants)
- Does not cover academic/research multi-agent environments (MARL, simulation)
- Not a build plan — this is landscape orientation only at this stage
- Not limited to any specific vertical (unless the user narrows scope)
- Does not cover agent monitoring/observability tooling as a primary focus

---

## Constraints

- Knowledge cutoff is August 2025 — any frameworks/products released after that will not appear in my base knowledge
- Must distinguish between frameworks (CrewAI, AutoGen, LangGraph) and vertical SaaS products built on them (11x, Artisan, AiSDR) — these are different classes
- Research must separate "demo projects" from production-grade, commercially deployed systems

---

## Assumptions

- The user is evaluating this space with an eye toward building or adopting, not just academic curiosity
- "Roles" means agent specialization (sales, ops, scheduling, finance, customer success, etc.) rather than permission scopes
- "Capture task" implies task creation, assignment, and lifecycle tracking — not just task execution
- The user has engineering capacity to implement something non-trivial

---

## Open Questions

- Are you interested in the **framework layer** (tools to build this yourself: CrewAI, AutoGen, LangGraph) or the **product layer** (companies that have already built vertical multi-agent systems)?
- Is there a specific **business function** in scope — e.g. sales outreach, customer ops, internal ops, finance, project management?
- Do you have an existing system (GSD, your openclaw stack, Taskwarrior) that agents would need to integrate with, or is this greenfield?
- What's the **deployment model** you're imagining — agents running 24/7 autonomously, or human-in-the-loop for decisions?
- Is **task capture** the hard problem here (structured intake of work), or is **task execution** (agents doing the work) the focus?

---

## Next Research Steps

1. Survey major multi-agent orchestration frameworks (CrewAI, AutoGen/Magentic-One, LangGraph, Agency Swarm) — architecture patterns, role assignment models, task routing mechanisms
2. Survey vertical AI agent products (11x, Artisan, AiSDR, Relevance AI, MultiOn) — what roles they implement, how tasks are captured and routed
3. Identify the common patterns: role definition schemas, task queue designs, handoff protocols, state persistence approaches
4. Identify failure modes and open problems: context loss across agents, conflicting agents, auditability, cost blow-up
5. Map against user's existing stack (GSD, openclaw, agent-stack) for integration surface area
