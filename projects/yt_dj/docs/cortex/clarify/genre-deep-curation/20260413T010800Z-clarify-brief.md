# Clarify Brief: genre-deep-curation

**Slug:** genre-deep-curation
**Timestamp:** 20260413T010800Z
**Status:** draft
**Complexity:** standard

---

## Idea

Yeah so the music collecting for genre's has been going quite well but how can I upgrade this with deep community knowledge and hidden gems artists and understand classics so we are covering that... not just surface level commercial hits and stuff like that...

---

## Goal

Build a systematic curation methodology and research pipeline that surfaces canonical classics, underground hidden gems, and community-validated deep cuts for each genus in the yt_dj music genome — ensuring every genus has representative tracks across all three tiers (classics, deep cuts, current underground) rather than only the most commercially visible releases.

---

## Non-Goals

- Not a replacement for the existing genome graph structure or D3.js visualisation
- Not automated scraping or bulk downloading — curation decisions remain manual and intentional
- Not a commercial playlist or streaming product — stays within the self-hosted radio context
- Not covering all 20 genera simultaneously in one pass — work genus by genus
- Not recreating existing large DJ mix archives (e.g., Boiler Room, RA podcast) as a discovery mechanism

---

## Constraints

- All tracks must be under 8 minutes (existing yt-dlp `--match-filter "duration < 480"` rule)
- Must be downloadable via yt-dlp with cookie auth — obscure tracks may not be on YouTube
- Library is SQLite-backed with BPM/camelot analysis; tracks must be mixable (not ambient spoken word, etc.)
- Bandwidth and storage are finite — target quality over quantity; ~10–15 deep cuts per genus max per pass
- yt_dj is a personal project, not a commercial service — rights/licensing not a concern for private use

---

## Assumptions

- The current genome has 20 genera, each with some library holdings but mostly weighted toward well-known artists
- "Deep community knowledge" means: record store staff picks, Discogs community lists, RA forum deep cuts, genre-specialist blogs and Substack newsletters, label discography deep dives
- "Hidden gems" are defined as tracks with <50k plays on YouTube or SoundCloud but high community esteem among genre specialists
- "Classics" are canonical tracks that any knowledgeable collector in the genre would consider essential — not necessarily commercially successful
- For each genus, there are 2–5 authoritative community sources (labels, blogs, forums, curators) that specialists cite as definitive

---

## Open Questions

- q1: For each of the 20 genera, who are the 3–5 authoritative community curators or label A&Rs whose taste is genre-defining? (type: landscape)
- q2: What are the canonical "desert island" tracks per genus — the ones every genre specialist would include? (type: factual)
- q3: What are the best community resources for each genus: label Bandcamp pages, Discogs lists, RA feature archives, genre Substack/blogs? (type: landscape)
- q4: Which genera in the current library are most undercovered or weakest relative to their depth of history? (type: codebase)
- q5: For the Lo-fi House genus specifically (most recently expanded), what tracks are considered essential by Lobster Theremin / Hessle Audio circles that we don't yet have? (type: factual)
- q6: How do underground tracks that never had a YouTube upload get discovered — SoundCloud private links, Bandcamp exclusives, physical-only releases? What's the discovery pathway? (type: mechanism)
- q7: What's the risk of over-homogenising a genre by only collecting community-validated picks — are there genuinely contrarian classics? (type: comparison)

---

## Next Research Steps

1. **Landscape survey per genus** (q1, q3) — For each of the 20 genera, identify the 3 most authoritative community voices / label pages / forums using Perplexity deep research
2. **Canonical classics per genus** (q2) — For the 5 weakest genera, research "essential tracks" lists from specialist sources (not Spotify/Apple Music charts)
3. **Library gap analysis** (q4) — Cross-reference current library holdings against community-curated essentials lists to identify coverage gaps per genus
4. **Underground discovery pathways** (q6) — Research how collectors in each scene surface tracks that haven't reached YouTube: Bandcamp new arrivals, label mailing lists, SoundCloud reposts by key curators
5. **Lo-fi House depth pass** (q5) — Specific research pass for LFH given recent 20-track expansion — what's the next tier that specialists would add?
6. **Contrarian check** (q7) — For 2–3 genera, identify tracks that are community classics but polarising — to ensure curation doesn't collapse into consensus
