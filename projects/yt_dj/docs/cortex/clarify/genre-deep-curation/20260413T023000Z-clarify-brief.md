# Clarify Brief: genre-deep-curation (iteration 2)

**Slug:** genre-deep-curation
**Timestamp:** 20260413T023000Z
**Status:** draft
**Complexity:** standard
**Iteration:** 2

---

## Idea

The music collecting for genres has been going well but needs upgrading with deep community knowledge and hidden gem artists — covering classics and depth, not just surface-level commercial hits. More specifically: every track proposed for the library should be defensible against community and critical sources of truth, not just found via YouTube search.

---

## Goal

Establish a validated curation methodology where every proposed track carries attached quality signals (critical record + practitioner signal + community validation), apply it genus-by-genus starting with the five most undercovered genera, AND actively identify adjacent or emerging genres that belong in the genome but aren't in it yet — so the library grows in both depth (within existing genera) and breadth (new genera added when warranted).

---

## Non-Goals

- Not automating track selection — human approval per batch remains the workflow
- Not sourcing from private/invite-only underground channels (anti-brand movement tier is acknowledged but out of scope)
- Not requiring all three quality signals simultaneously — minimum two of three, flagged when only one
- Not retroactively re-evaluating the 168 tracks already in the library
- Not adding a new genus without proposing at least 5 anchor tracks with quality signals alongside the proposal — a genus without defensible holdings is just a label

---

## Constraints

- All tracks must pass the 8-minute duration pre-flight check before proposing
- yt-dlp downloadable — Bandcamp tracks work directly; Discogs physical-only releases require a YouTube upload to exist
- Track proposals must follow the defined format: Artist — Title [Label, Year] + Critical + Practitioner + Community + Duration
- One genus per pass — not a broad multi-genus sweep

---

## Assumptions

- The three quality signals (critical / practitioner / community) are sufficient proxies for "deep community knowledge" without requiring active participation in private collector networks
- RA Essential Albums + Discogs wants >200 in agreement = strong enough validation for most electronic genera
- The Wire Magazine is the authoritative critical source for IDM, Ambient, and experimental — more so than Pitchfork for these genera
- For world music genera (Afrobeat, Ethnic, Global Funk), Songlines and label liner notes (Finders Keepers, Now-Again, Mr Bongo) are the equivalent of RA
- Bandcamp Daily monthly roundups are a reliable editorial pipeline for all 20 genera going back to 2016

---

## Open Questions

- q1 (factual): For the five critical genera, what are 2–3 specific RA Essential Album reviews or Wire Magazine features that define the canon? (These become the starting reading list per genus.)
- q2 (factual): What is the Discogs wants threshold that reliably separates "collector-validated classic" from "well-known but not canonical"? Is 200 the right number or does it vary by genre?
- q3 (mechanism): How do 1001tracklists.com and Boiler Room tracklist archives work as practitioner signal sources — can they be searched by track title to find which DJs have played something?
- q4 (codebase): Does the existing `analyze_library.py` / `track_metadata.json` schema have room for a `quality_signals` field, or should that live in a separate curation notes file?
- q5 (landscape): What genres exist in 2020–2026 that are musically adjacent to the current 20 genera but aren't represented? Candidates: Footwork/Juke, Gqom, Jersey Club, Kuduro, Hauntology, Outsider House, UK Drill-adjacent electronic, Deconstructed Club — which have enough community depth and library-compatible tracks to warrant a new genus node?
- q6 (comparison): For candidate new genera, what's the bar for adding a node to the genome vs treating it as a sub-genus of an existing node? What makes Footwork a distinct genus rather than a sub-node under IDM?

---

## Next Research Steps

1. **Per-genus reading list** (q1) — For Ambient, Afrobeat, Balearic, Afro-Cosmic, IDM: identify the 1–2 canonical RA/Wire/Songlines reviews that define what's essential. These become the briefing document before each genus pass.
2. **Discogs threshold calibration** (q2) — Research whether the >200 wants threshold holds across genres or needs adjustment (e.g., niche genres where 50 wants = very strong signal)
3. **Tracklist search mechanics** (q3) — Document the 1001tracklists.com search interface and Boiler Room archive as practitioner signal tools
4. **Schema extension** (q4) — Check whether quality signals belong in the DB or in a sidecar markdown file per genus
5. **Emerging genre landscape survey** (q5) — Survey 2020–2026 electronic music scenes for genera candidates missing from the genome; evaluate each against the bar: distinct sound, clear label ecosystem, 5+ library-compatible tracks available
6. **Genus addition bar** (q6) — Define what distinguishes a new genus node from a sub-genus: lineage break, geographic distinctness, label ecosystem independence, community self-identification as separate
