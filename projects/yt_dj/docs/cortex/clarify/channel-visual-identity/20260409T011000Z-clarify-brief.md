---

## Goal

A cohesive channel identity and visual system for an anonymous 24/7 YouTube live stream that functions as a "portal to the world right now" — pairing underground electronic music with diverse live visual sources (webcams, nature cams, space feeds, weather) to create an ambient, contemplative, always-on window into the planet.

---

## Non-Goals

- Not building a personal brand or personality-driven channel
- Not a music discovery platform with track listings or artist promotions
- Not interactive beyond basic chatbot commands (no polls, no viewer requests in v1)
- Not a news or information channel — no text overlays with news/weather data
- Not a high-production-value video channel (no custom animations, no motion graphics in v1)

---

## Constraints

- Must work within the existing FFmpeg/OBS pipeline — visual enhancements must be compositable in real time
- Must use only publicly accessible, legally reusable visual sources
- Must maintain the "live" feeling — no pre-recorded video loops, no obviously synthetic content
- Must be anonymous — no attribution to the owner, no personal branding
- Visual sources must be reliable enough for 24/7 operation (fallback logic for dead feeds)
- Must not increase CPU load beyond what the server can handle alongside the audio pipeline

---

## Assumptions

- There are enough diverse, high-quality public webcam and live feed sources to keep the visuals interesting 24/7
- Viewers will stay for the ambient combination of live visuals + music, not for any single element
- Time-of-day awareness (showing nighttime cams when it's night somewhere) would significantly improve the experience
- Simple text overlays (location, time) are sufficient — no complex graphics needed
- The channel philosophy can be communicated through the visuals and music selection alone, without explicit branding or mission statements

---

## Open Questions

- What live visual sources exist beyond Windy webcams? (ISS feed, underwater cams, volcano cams, aurora cams, airport runway cams, surf cams, train cams, wildlife cams)
- How to implement time-of-day awareness — show daytime cams from places where it's daytime, night cams from nighttime zones?
- What visual layout best serves the "portal" concept? (Full-screen single cam with slow transitions? Grid? Picture-in-picture? Rotating single focus?)
- Should there be any text overlay at all? (Location name, local time, "LIVE" indicator?)
- What's the channel name, description, and branding aesthetic? (Minimal? Abstract? Geographic?)
- How to curate visual themes that pair with music moods? (e.g., ocean cams with ambient, city cams with house)
- What makes viewers feel "this is happening right now" vs "this is a loop"?
- Are there other live YouTube streams / channels with a similar philosophy to study?

---

## Next Research Steps

1. Survey all available live visual source types — webcams, nature cams, space, underwater, transit, weather, wildlife — with API/stream access
2. Research existing ambient/portal-style YouTube channels and their visual approaches
3. Investigate time-of-day aware cam selection — timezone APIs, solar position, dawn/dusk detection
4. Explore visual layout options for the "portal" feeling — transitions, focus modes, overlays
5. Research minimal branding/identity approaches for anonymous ambient channels
6. Assess which visual sources pair best with which music moods
7. Evaluate FFmpeg/OBS capabilities for dynamic visual transitions (dissolve, fade, ken burns on stills)
