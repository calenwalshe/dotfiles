# Research Dossier: channel-visual-identity — concept

**Depth:** standard

## Summary

The channel should be a **single full-screen window that follows the sun around the globe**, crossfading between live feeds with slow 3-5 second dissolves. Not a grid — grids feel like surveillance. The strongest ambient streams feel like infrastructure, not content: a window you leave open. The visual identity is the planet itself, rotating through dawn, day, golden hour, dusk, night, city lights. The music is the emotional layer; the visuals are the geographic layer. Minimal text overlay (location + local time, 60% opacity). Anonymous, no brand, no host. The name should be descriptive and portal-like ("Window Seat" is taken, but the pattern is clear). Solar position calculation drives feed selection — prefer golden hour feeds for maximum visual impact.

## Findings

### Channel Philosophy

- The strongest 24/7 ambient streams feel like **infrastructure, not content** — a utility, a window you leave open
- No host, no personality. The world is the content. Anonymous by design.
- "Portal" language recurs: "window," "real-time," "live," "around the world." The framing is about access, not production.
- Most viewers are **not actively watching** — the stream is a desktop companion for work, study, sleep
- Chat community is light, ambient — shared quiet presence, not active discussion
- The less "produced" it feels, the more trustworthy and habitual it becomes

### Visual Layout: Single Cam, Not Grid

- **Single cam full-screen with slow crossfade is the strongest choice for ambient.** Grids feel like surveillance, split attention, fight the meditative mood.
- Rotate between locations every 2-5 minutes with 3-5 second dissolves
- Grid can be used sparingly as a "global dashboard" beat every 15-20 minutes
- Picture-in-picture with upcoming feed thumbnail fading in builds anticipation before the switch
- Ken Burns effect (slow pan/zoom on stills) prevents static frames from feeling dead

### The Killer Feature: Follow the Sun

- **Sun-chasing narrative** turns a webcam feed into a story about the planet rotating
- Viewers in New York see Tokyo's morning, then Dubai's afternoon, then their own sunset
- Use `pvlib.solarposition.get_solarposition(timestamp, lat, lon)` to compute solar elevation per camera
- Solar elevation > 0 = daytime, < -6 = civil twilight, < -12 = night
- **Prefer golden hour feeds** (elevation 0-10 degrees) for maximum visual impact
- Rotate selection every 10-15 minutes based on solar position

### Liveness Markers (What Makes It Feel Real)

1. Local time overlay updating in real-time proves the feed is current
2. Visible weather/movement (rain, traffic, clouds, waves) that cannot be faked
3. Following the sun around the globe — the temporal progression is the proof
4. Real sunrise/sunset are the main "events" — viewers stay for these natural transitions

### Overlay Design

- **Lower-left:** city name in thin sans-serif, local time underneath, both at ~60% opacity white with subtle drop shadow
- **Upper-right:** small pulsing red dot + "LIVE" in caps
- No borders, no boxes, no backgrounds. Let the feed breathe.
- Fade in/out, nothing persistent

### Music-Visual Pairing

- Higher energy tracks → urban/daytime feeds
- Ambient pads → water, fog, twilight
- Tempo drives transition speed: slow BPM = longer crossfades (5-8s), uptempo = quicker (2-3s)
- Rhythm changes are natural transition trigger points

### Live Visual Sources

**Tier 1 — Public domain / freely reusable:**
- NASA ISS Earth View (public domain, HD-4K, 24/7 with ~10% blue screen during handovers)
- U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service refuge cams (federal, public domain)
- explore.org partner streams (many explicitly allow embedding)

**Tier 2 — Public webcams via API:**
- Windy Webcams API (73,000+ cams, REST API, JPEG snapshots + HLS)
- State DOT traffic cameras (public data, JPEG refresh)
- Helios Cameras API (US DOT, REST + HLS)

**Tier 3 — Specialty sources (licensing varies):**
- Aurora cams: Star Lapland, Lights Over Lapland (YouTube streams, seasonal Sep-Mar)
- Surf cams: livebeaches.com, 805webcams (web embed)
- Airport/aviation: tabi.cam (30+ airports), MiamiAirportCam (HD + ATC audio)
- Underwater: Monterey Bay Aquarium, Ocean Networks Canada
- Wildlife: San Diego Zoo, explore.org network
- Train cab rides: Norwegian railway, Stockholm tram (pre-recorded loops, not truly live)

**Automation APIs:**
- auroras.live — Kp index to trigger aurora cam switching
- Open-Meteo Marine API — wave conditions for coastal cam selection
- pvlib — solar position for time-of-day awareness

## Trade-offs

### Option: Single Cam Full-Screen with Sun-Chasing Rotation
**Pros:** Most immersive, meditative, "window" feeling. Follows the sun as narrative. Proven by Window Seat, Slow TV. Simple to implement (one feed at a time + dissolve transition).
**Cons:** Less visually "busy" — some viewers may find it too slow. Single point of failure if the active feed dies.
**Verdict:** selected — with fallback to next-best feed on failure.

### Option: Grid Layout (Current Approach)
**Pros:** Shows multiple locations simultaneously. More "information density."
**Cons:** Feels like surveillance or CCTV. Splits attention. Fights ambient mood. Research unanimously says single > grid for this concept.
**Verdict:** rejected as primary — keep as occasional "dashboard" moment every 15-20 minutes.

### Option: Hybrid (Single Focus + Small PIP Preview)
**Pros:** Shows what's coming next. Builds anticipation.
**Cons:** Slightly more complex. Could distract from the main view.
**Verdict:** deferred — nice enhancement for v2 but not needed for initial identity.

---

## Recommendations

- **Switch from grid to single full-screen cam** with slow 3-5s dissolve transitions
- **Implement sun-chasing rotation** using pvlib solar position — prefer golden hour, follow the dawn/dusk line around the planet
- **Minimal overlay:** lower-left location + local time (thin sans-serif, 60% opacity), upper-right pulsing red "LIVE" dot
- **Add NASA ISS feed** as a special rotating source (public domain, spectacular visuals)
- **Rotate every 3-5 minutes** — long enough to settle in, short enough to maintain variety
- **Channel name** should be descriptive and portal-like: "Now, Somewhere" / "Earth Window" / "The View From Here" / "Right Now" — nothing branded
- **Channel description:** one line. "Live views from around the world. Underground electronic music. Always on."
- **Pair music mood with visual context** — ambient tracks during twilight/ocean, deeper house during city night scenes

## Adjacent Findings

- **NASA ISS Earth View feed is public domain and freely reusable:** NASA media policy explicitly permits reuse of NASA-originated footage. This is the only HD/4K live feed from space that can be legally composited into a YouTube stream without licensing. It adds a visual dimension (orbital perspective) that no webcam can match and strongly reinforces the "portal to the world" concept. Availability is ~90% (blue screen during satellite handovers). Source: NASA media usage guidelines, nasa.gov.
