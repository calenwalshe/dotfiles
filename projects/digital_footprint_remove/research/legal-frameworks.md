# Legal Frameworks for Data Removal

## California Delete Act (SB 362) & DROP

**Status**: Infrastructure live as of 2026

- **DROP (Delete Request Online Portal)**: state-hosted portal for California consumers to submit bulk deletion requests to registered data brokers
- **Broker obligation**: Starting August 1, 2026, registered data brokers must check DROP at least every 45 days and process deletion requests
- **Exceptions**: Some categories of data/brokers are exempt (details TBD by enforcement)
- **Significance**: First state-level infrastructure that shifts the burden from consumer-to-each-broker to a centralized mechanism

## Global Privacy Control (GPC)

- Browser-based HTTP signal indicating opt-out preference
- Recognized as a valid opt-out under California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA)
- Supported natively in Firefox; available via extensions in other browsers
- Reduces new data collection but does not trigger deletion of existing data
- Legal weight is expanding — other states considering recognition

## CCPA / CPRA (California)

- Right to know what data is collected
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt out of sale/sharing
- Applies to businesses meeting revenue/data thresholds
- GPC signal satisfies opt-out requirement

## GDPR (EU/EEA)

- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — Article 17
- Applies to any organization processing EU resident data
- Exceptions: legal obligations, public interest, freedom of expression
- Relevant for EU-based accounts or services with EU operations

## Google-Specific Removal Policies

- Will remove results containing personal contact info (phone, address, government IDs)
- "Results about you" tool enables proactive monitoring
- Outdated content removal available when source has changed
- Does not guarantee removal — reviewed case by case

## Practical Limitations of Legal Mechanisms

1. **Compliance records**: Companies may retain minimal records proving they processed your deletion request
2. **Public records**: Government records (property, court, voter registration) are generally exempt
3. **Breach datasets**: Data already leaked in breaches exists outside any legal framework's practical reach
4. **Third-party copies**: Content reposted by others may not be covered by deletion of original
5. **Archive policies**: Internet Archive reviews removal requests individually, no blanket compliance
